DIGITAL DEBRI

Year: 2022
Medium: Video Installation
Exhibition: Espaço Porta-Jazz, Porto, Portugal

Digital Debri is an exploration of digital decay and the ephemeral nature of media. This video installation uses vintage CRT monitors to display manipulated footage that examines how digital information breaks down over time, creating new forms and meanings from degradation.

Originally exhibited at Espaço Porta-Jazz in Porto as part of the "Depressa vai o tempo que vem" exhibition (October-December 2022), the installation features a stack of CRT television sets displaying synchronized and distorted video content. The work creates a dialogue between digital technology and memory, questioning our relationship with digital archives and the permanence of information.

Using techniques of video manipulation, signal interference, and data degradation, Digital Debri transforms once-clear images into abstract patterns that challenge viewers to find meaning in corruption. The work serves as a meditation on technological obsolescence and the materiality of digital media in an age of virtual impermanence.

The installation's composition with stacked television sets creates a sculptural presence that contrasts the physicality of obsolete hardware with the intangibility of digital content, highlighting our complex relationship with technology as both artifact and medium.
